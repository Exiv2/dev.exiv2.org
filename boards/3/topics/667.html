Adding Exiv2 to a bootable Linux ("LiveCD") CD or image -- possible?

Added by Steve Wright about 11 years ago

This question is prompted by a script issue I have, which I intend to discuss in another thread.

Just off the top of my head, I suppose if one had an ISO of, say, Ubuntu 10.4 or Puppy Linux, one could copy the contents of it to a folder and do a configure/make/make install, editing the relevant files so instead of using your Linux or Windows (MinGW/Cygwin) install paths, it installed to the ones inside the folder. Then it'd be a matter of trial and error whether or not, once burned to a CD and in a session started by booting from same, the executables and libs/so's "got the hint" and ran as if they were on an HDD-installed Linux.

Has anyone ever tried it? Or better, does anyone know of a repository or archive where one could find images with Exiv2 (of some recent vintage though the latest would OC be the preferred choice) already among the "available extras"?
