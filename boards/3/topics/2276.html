RE: Initial use of exiv2 - Added by Robin Mills about 6 years ago

Dave

I apologise for being so rude to you. I can't imagine what I was thinking. I'm normally patient and try to be helpful. As Andreas has said, we'll help you with Exiv2 related stuff. When you open the zip, it'll run exiv2.exe in a DOS window and exit. Not very helpful. What you should do is to extract it into a directory such as c:\Users\yourname\exiv2. Then launch cmd.exe:

c:\> cd \Users\yourname\exiv2
c:\Users\yourname\exiv2\> exiv2 -h
And you'll get some information about options for the program. In the first instance, you'll almost certainly want to examine the metadata in an image on your computer. The default option lists a summary:
c:\Users\yourname\exiv2\> exiv2 ..\Pictures\foo.jpg
The option -pa lists all the metadata.
exiv2 -pa ..\Pictures\foo.jpg
Once you have the program working, you can explore some of the options. A good read at the man page might put you to sleep, however you'll discover that we have options to read, modify and delete metadata in images of many types. http://www.exiv2.org/manpage.html

Image metadata, like most things in software, is a more complex field than you might suspect. There are 3 common standards supported by exiv2: Exif, IPTC and XMP. When I take a photo with my Nikon D5300, it typically stores about 160 items such as date/time, flash, lens, aperture, speed, focus, geo-location, camera model, preview image and lots more. Oh, and it stores the image itself of course.

RE: Initial use of exiv2 - Added by Robin Mills about 6 years ago

Thanks, Dave. You'll find there are lots of nice (free) programs such as Picasa, Adobe Bridge and DigiKam which provide metadata GUIs. The exiv2 project's mission is provide the low-level library to be used by such applications. DigiKam includes libexiv2. We haven't been successful yet in persuading Google and Adobe to adopt libexiv2.

The application exiv2.exe is used by our test suite and can exercise almost every feature of our code base. Exiv2.exe is a useful application in it own right, however you've got to be a command-line guy to like this kind of thing. We will never going provide a GUI for libexiv2 as the focus of our effort is to provide a robust and reliable library. Developing GUIs is fun and time consuming. We choose to keep out of that area.

Good Luck and thank you for chatting on the Forum.
