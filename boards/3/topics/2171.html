RE: Exporting Exif integer tags - Added by Robin Mills over 6 years ago

Mikayel

I'm not sure I understand your question. http://dev.exiv2.org/attachments/download/805/DSC_7154.jpg

If you print the value with -pa they will be 'translated' (displayed in human form).

$ exiv2 -pa -g Resol http://dev.exiv2.org/attachments/download/805/DSC_7154.jpg
Exif.Image.XResolution                       Rational    1  300
Exif.Image.YResolution                       Rational    1  300
Exif.Image.ResolutionUnit                    Short       1  inch
Exif.Thumbnail.XResolution                   Rational    1  300
Exif.Thumbnail.YResolution                   Rational    1  300
Exif.Thumbnail.ResolutionUnit                Short       1  inch
$
If you use -pv you will see the 'vanilla values'. So 2 is inch (and the default).
$ exiv2 -pv -g Resol http://dev.exiv2.org/attachments/download/805/DSC_7154.jpg
0x011a Image        XResolution                 Rational    1  300/1
0x011b Image        YResolution                 Rational    1  300/1
0x0128 Image        ResolutionUnit              Short       1  2
0x011a Thumbnail    XResolution                 Rational    1  300/1
0x011b Thumbnail    YResolution                 Rational    1  300/1
0x0128 Thumbnail    ResolutionUnit              Short       1  2
$
If you're asking "can I specify 'inch'?" and Exiv2 will store a Short 2, I believe the answer is no. If you're asking the question, "If I change the ResolutionUnit Tag from 2 to 1, will the relevant XResolution and YResolution be modified automatically, again I believe the answer is no.

Please understand that Exiv2 is technology for working with metadata. If applications want to provide features to change units and the like, they can deal with this kind of auto-magic. However the focus of Exiv2 is to provide reliable capability. And with great strength, comes great responsibility. Exiv2 is not a metadata policeman who enforces consistency - that is the responsibility of the application using the library.
