Subsecond in rename?

Added by tim carlin over 12 years ago

exiv2 -pv reports the subsecond data from an image.

I would like to rename files based on the date including the subsecond data.

I see how to use strftime(3) but it doesn't appear to have subsecond support.

Suggestions? Thanks.

RE: Subsecond in rename? - Added by Robin Mills over 12 years ago

Tim

I was a little surprised by your question because I wasn't aware that exiv2 (nor the exif specification) supported this. Indeed it's in the spec (http://www.exif.org/Exif2-2.PDF) however I don't believe any of my 4 digital cameras support those tags.

strftime doesn't support sub seconding timing. However that's a matter for the Standard "C" Library Committee to ponder.

However I have a suggestion:

If you're renaming files based on time, then in general most folks don't take more than 1 photo in a second. So there's no problem - let's say you have photos such as this:
520 /Users/rmills/Pictures/Photos/2005/AlansWedding/Reception> exiv2 -pv Husky\ and\ Katya.jpg | grep -i time
0x0132 Image DateTime Ascii 20 2005:08:06 07:16:48
0x829a Photo ExposureTime Rational 1 1/100
0x9003 Photo DateTimeOriginal Ascii 20 2005:08:06 07:16:48
0x9004 Photo DateTimeDigitized Ascii 20 2005:08:06 07:16:48
0x0002 CanonCs Selftimer Short 1 0
521 /Users/rmills/Pictures/Photos/2005/AlansWedding/Reception> exiv2 -pv Husky\ and\ Katya.jpg | grep -i sec
522 /Users/rmills/Pictures/Photos/2005/AlansWedding/Reception>
(Alan's wedding wasn't at 8am - the camera was still in PDT (California) and the Wedding was in England! - the photo was taken at 4pm)

You simply name it 2005:08:06 07:16:48.jpg. If you find you have more than 1 with the same name, you name them 2005:08:06 07:16:48_1.jpg ..._2.jpg. If you have sub second timing, well you take advantage of that when you sort and name - however in general I think you will not. strftime can create the date string to which you have to append ".jpg" anyway. So you can also add the _sub-second data (or simply invent it). The same dodge can be applied to multiple photos that collide at the subsecond resolution (presumably from a very high speed camera).

One of the consequences of this date format is that sorting by name is the same as sort by date! I personally enjoy photos more when they appear in chronological sequence. Your taste may differ.

Hope that helps.

Robin

RE: Subsecond in rename? - Added by Andreas Huggel over 12 years ago

Tim,

I see you've found a solution that works for you; good. So just for the sake of completeness:

The exiv2 rename command currently only has the -F option to deal with such cases. It does what Robin suggested, i.e, appends _1, _2, etc. to the filename to avoid duplicates. The requested feature is not something that strftime can do, as you pointed out. I'd have to add it explicitly, maybe in the form of a :subsec: option similar to how :basename: works.

Andreas
