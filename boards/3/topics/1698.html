Approximate values in DateTimeOriginal

Added by Anonymous Poster almost 8 years ago

I'm currently scanning a lot of old slides, and in many cases I don't known the exact date when the picture was taken. Sometimes I known the month, sometimes only the year.

What is the best value to store in Exif.Photo.DateTimeOriginal in those cases? I'm tempted to set the date (and sometimes the month) to zero to indicate an unknown date/month, but is that permitted by the standard, or am I inviting trouble by doing so? May I use question marks for unknown values?

/Claus

RE: Approximate values in DateTimeOriginal - Added by Robin Mills almost 8 years ago

Claus

I'm not sure there is an answer to your question. I don't recall the specification of dates in metadata and www.exif.org seems to be down. However, I think messing with the date and using ? in place of digits is asking for workflow trouble. If the date is specified as an "iso date" - it should conform. I suggest you use midnight on the first of the month or year because that's what you know. This is legal syntax and will not disrupt other software.

Robin
