FireFox extension tester plese

Added by Samuel Turner about 12 years ago

I have a FireFox extension which uses the exiv2 binary and was hoping I might get some extra interest in this forum from people who would see reason to use it, I need it tested and there's nothing quite like feedback. Here's what it does:

What happens when you download an image from the internet normally? It saves to your computer as "gdgd5343jeeb.jpg" and is completely useless with search tools because you can't ever find it again.

To fix this I have an extension called "met.a" which embeds relevant metadata into the images you download automatically so you can find them again. That means you still get the same "gdgd5343jeeb.jpg" image, but inside it will hold information like:

1. URL: The URL so that when you come back to the image in the future it will be possible to simply see that webpage you got it from, giving you an idea why you wanted it in the first place and where you got it from. It puts the image into context.

2. Webpage Title: Often webpage titles convey everything you need, like The Times Online, BBC UK News... often you'll remember an image with the overall reason why you downloaded it e.g. new Hubble Telescope photos, and because this webpage title data is stored inside the image its then possible to search by this information which makes finding images a lot easier.

3. Highlighted text: Often blocks of text are either associated or relevant to an image on a webpage, by simply selecting text on a webpage this will also be embedded into the image along with the other data and used as search criteria.

met.a is in beta right now and works on Mac OS X (PPC/Intel), Windows Vista and Linux so lots of people can try it, I really need feedback to know how to improve it and how to squash bugs. It basically adds in the information from the webpage into the image itself so you can find it again once its downloaded onto your computer while helping you understand why you got it in the first place and from where.

I hope you like it.
