RE: Type Undefined or Byte in Exif.Photo.ComponentsConfiguration - Added by Robin Mills about 7 years ago

Thomas

I think the issue is in the file and therefore a Samsung issue. Here is my analysis.

I had no difficulty reproducing your result:

1309 rmills@rmillsmbp:~/temp $ curl -O http://dev.exiv2.org/attachments/679/20140915_170208.jpg
1310 rmills@rmillsmbp:~/temp $ exiv2 -g Components -pt 20140915_170208.jpg 
Exif.Photo.ComponentsConfiguration           Byte        4  YCbCr

I looked at the definition in tags.cpp#1612
        TagInfo(0x9101, "ComponentsConfiguration", N_("Components Configuration"),
                N_("Information specific to compressed data. The channels of " 
                "each component are arranged in order from the 1st " 
                "component to the 4th. For uncompressed data the data " 
                "arrangement is given in the <PhotometricInterpretation> tag. " 
                "However, since <PhotometricInterpretation> can only " 
                "express the order of Y, Cb and Cr, this tag is provided " 
                "for cases when compressed data uses components other than " 
                "Y, Cb, and Cr and to enable support of other sequences."),
                exifId, imgConfig, undefined, 4, print0x9101),

So my thought was, there's something in the file to say "this is Byte". So, I processed the file with Phil's wonderful Exiftool:
1320 rmills@rmillsmbp:~/temp $ exiftool -htmlDump 20140915_170208.jpg | grep ComponentsConfig
<a name=t26 class=W>ExifIFD-07 ComponentsConfiguration</a>
<div id=p26 class=tt><b>ExifIFD-07 ComponentsConfiguration</b><br>Tag ID: 0x9101<br>Format: int8u[4]<br>Size: 4 bytes<br>Value: 1 2 3 0</div>
1321 rmills@rmillsmbp:~/temp $

And looking at the HTML in the browser, I see:
0126   01 91 01 00 04 00 00 00 01 02 03 00  | ............ | ExifIFD-07 ComponentsConfiguration

You can see that Exiftool says it's an array of 4 int8u's. Presumably, there's something to say I'm a Byte array (probably the 01) as 91 01 looks like the tag. 04 is obviously the length and 01 02 03 00 are the values in the byte array.

The exif2 spec states on page 24 (http://www.kodak.com/global/plugins/acrobat/en/service/digCam/exifStandard2.pdf)

C. Tags Relating to Image Configuration
￼￼￼￼￼￼￼￼ComponentsConfiguration 0x9101 type = UNDEFINED count = 4

Page 14, states:

The following types are used in Exif:
1 = BYTE
2 = ASCII
3 = SHORT
4 = LONG
5 = RATIONAL
7 = UNDEFINED
9 = SLONG
10 = SRATIONAL

Count
An 8-bit unsigned integer.,

So ... abracadabra ... Samsung have a 1 when perhaps they should have a 7.

Of course, Andreas, or Niels or Phil may further comment on this as they are experts in metadata. I'm the build manager for Exiv2 and don't know much about metadata.

Robin
