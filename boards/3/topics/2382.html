Extracting TIF preview from Canon CR2 files levels compressed / colour distorted

Added by Matthew Malthouse over 5 years ago

Canon CR2 files have 3 embedded thumbnail images:

matthew@nine:/tmp$ exiv2 -pp /data/camera/2007/20070930/IMG_7119.cr2
Preview 1: image/jpeg, 160x120 pixels, 9008 bytes
Preview 2: image/tiff, 384x256 pixels, 294912 bytes
Preview 3: image/jpeg, 1936x1288 pixels, 681240 bytes

For my purposes the tiff would be the ideal size (and exiv2's extraction super efficient) but while the small and large jpgs are fine the tiff is murky dark and green tinged.

Actually a histogram shows that all the image data is compressed into the range 0-153 — or the darker 60% of the full 0-255 levels in each of the RGB channels.

Can anyone tell me if this is a property of the CR2 embedded TIFF or if it an artefact of the extraction by exiv2?

I can't imagine why Canon might play with the Tiff (although I've learned not to be surprised by anything in this game) but if they have then the most efficient fix for me will be to use the large thumbnail and convert it to an appropriate size. However if this is something to do with exiv2 then I shall be asking further how to fix it. If it's neither...

exiv2 0.23 001700 (64 bit build) on Ubuntu 14.04 x86_64 SMp kernel 3.13.0-79-generic

Here's an extracted tiff preview — the tents should be close to white and the trees green! — and it's histogram.

RE: Extracting TIF preview from Canon CR2 files levels compressed / colour distorted - Added by Robin Mills over 5 years ago

Gosh, Matthew. This is a surprise.

The focus of Exiv2 is to provide reliable/robust code to read/modify/write metadata. The meaning of the metadata isn't our concern. You are asking about the histogram of the embedded image in a CR2. I assure you that our code is neutral. Embedded images and most of the metadata are treated as binary blobs from our point of view. If you believe we are distorting that data, I will investigate.

RE: Extracting TIF preview from Canon CR2 files levels compressed / colour distorted - Added by Matthew Malthouse over 5 years ago

I was surprised too! :)

I'm not anywhere near pointing fingers, just asking if anyone had certain knowledge about the state of the CR2 format tiff preview and this "problem".

I realised that I was pretty random in my trials yesterday so I was more methodical this morning. Tiff thumbnails from a Canon 350D are poor, a 400D worse (those are both 8-bit) and a 70D (a 16-bit tiff) terrible with all the available data squeezed into the darkest 20% of the available gammut.

It seems my google-foo had also deserted me yesterday as I couldn't find anything pertinent but can today, like this from which:
The TIFF preview also appears to be rendered linearly, with no gamma correction applied, and the white and black points set at the extremes of the sensor's dynamic range.

So it looks as if the cause is with the original data and nothing whatsoever to do with Exiv2.

This is very irritating because I'm writing a script to extract previews from ~20k images to make an easily browsable catalogue and pulling the larger jpeg and resizing it, while technically easy with a touch of Imagemagik, is going to seriously compromise the efficiency inherent in Exiv2 which I found capable of extracting all those previews in seconds. This makes me sad.

RE: Extracting TIF preview from Canon CR2 files levels compressed / colour distorted - Added by Robin Mills over 5 years ago

Thanks for the update. I can't say that your message caused me to loose sleep, however I couldn't imagine how Exiv2 could have done this!

The good news is that changing the histogram "linearisation" requires almost no computational effort and as thumbnails are small, there aren't many pixels. I suspect you can extract/correct 50 thumbnails/second (4k/minute). If however you have to resample the original image, fast it will never be (4/minute).
